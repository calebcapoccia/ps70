<div class="textcontainer typewriter-texture"> <p class="margin"> </p> <h3>Week 7: Electronic Outputs</h3> <p>I am pursuing the "Kachow" (final name TBD) project idea that I had proposed. The current goal for the final system will be two displays affixed one each to the front and rear dashboards, and a mobile app/site to communicate with the displays.</p> <p>One of the most challenging aspects of the project is ensuring I can get speech to text (STT) working properly and can transmit that message to the LED display, so in addition to a fairly robust housing, this was the big thing to start to tackle in the MVP.</p> <h4>1. Input, Output, and Housing/Enclosure</h4> <h5>Input</h5> <p>The input device for my MVP is the microphone on a smartphone. The smartphone also will perform speech to text processing before transmitting the message via wireless signal to an ESP32 microcontroller. Additional inputs for the final version will be buttons on each of the displays to power on and off the devices (as I hope to use rechargeable batteries). For the MVP I had originally considered using an I2S MEMS microphone and performing the STT processing on the microcontroller, but as Nathan noted, a library capable of this would require significant compute, on the order of a Raspberry Pi, so would not be practical. I opted for an ESP32 Devkit V1 for its WiFi capabilities. Additionally, given that I want to end up using a phone app, it would be better to start with that as opposed to trying to wrangle with the I2S.</p> <h5>Output</h5> <p>The output device is a 64x8 LED display. I purchased two 32x8 <a href="https://www.analog.com/media/en/technical-documentation/data-sheets/max7219-max7221.pdf" target="_blank">MAX7219</a> displays and soldered wires from the end of one to another to daisy chain the display.</p> <p>For housing, I opted to laser cut a plywood box, as I will likely end up milling the final housing, but I do not have training on the CNC mill yet. I measured the soldered display, as well as the ESP32 and the USB-C cord I was using for it to incorporate those measurements in the housing. To hold the box together I used finger joints along with a healthy amount of gorilla glue.</p> <img src="./week7_1.jpg" alt="Laser cut housing" style="max-width: 600px;"> <p>To hold the display in place within the housing, I opted to 3D print clamps that I could slide onto the ends of the display and then affix with nuts and M3 8mm bolts to the housing. I opted to use M3 25mm bolts to sufficiently hold the ESP32 in place, but did not 3D print additional support, as I might switch microcontrollers. For the clamps, I had to perform very precise measurements and went through multiple iterations before ending up with a design that would fit the display and leave room for the pins.</p> <p>The iterations:</p> <img src="./week7_2.jpg" alt="Clamp iterations" style="max-width: 600px;"> <p>The final:</p> <img src="./week7_3.jpg" alt="Final clamp design" style="max-width: 600px;"> <div style="width:100%; height:60vh; max-height:600px;"> <iframe id="vs_iframe" src="https://www.viewstl.com/?embedded&url=https://calebcapoccia.github.io/ps70/07_outputs/mvp_clamps.stl" style="border:0;margin:0;width:100%;height:100%;" loading="lazy"> </iframe> </div> <img src="./week7_4.jpg" alt="Clamp installed" style="max-width: 600px;"> <p>In the final product, I might want clamps for additional support in the center of the display, but these were sufficient for the MVP.</p> <p>After constructing the box, it was time to start programming!</p> <img src="./week7_5.jpg" alt="Box construction" style="max-width: 600px;"> <img src="./week7_6.jpg" alt="Box assembly" style="max-width: 600px;"> <img src="./week7_7.jpg" alt="Completed housing" style="max-width: 600px;"> <p class="margin"> </p> <div class="flexrow"> <a id="btn" href="./mvp_clamps.stl" download>Download Clamp STL File </a> </div> <p class="margin"> </p> <p class="margin"> </p> <div class="flexrow"> <a id="btn" href="./mvp.f3d" download>Download Fusion Housing File </a> </div> <p class="margin"> </p> <h4>2. Programming</h4> <p>The programming portion of the MVP is made up of both the microcontroller programming, as well as the application that the acts as the intermediary between the user and the microcontroller. For communication and STT, I thought through a number of potential approaches, having a conversation with my friend Chet (ChatGPT). Given that my main goal for the MVP was to establish communication between the two devices, I opted to utilize the ESP32's ability to connect to WiFi and transmit information over a WiFi network. In the future, I might explore BLE (bluetooth) for communication. For STT, to keep things simple, I am using the built in speech recognition functionality within browsers. In the future, I might move to using Whisper, or another more robust STT library.</p> <p>Jumping into the code, I first worked towards displaying a simple message on the display.
